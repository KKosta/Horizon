Overview

From who we vote for and how we travel, to what we eat, energy is topic that surrounds our everyday life. It is often the centre of focus for the international media and political debate. Therefore we decided that for Warwick Engineering Society's first conference a suitable title would be "The Future of Energy", as we try to discover how society will tackle the energy challenges of the future.

At a university that has been fortunate enough to host some fantastic student led conferences like The Economics Summit, TEDx and The Leadership Summit, Warwick Engineering Society now proudly presents the newest edition to this esteemed line up.

On the 25th January, Warwick Energy Conference will welcome over 200 attendees from across the globe to discuss, analyse and debate the topic of energy in all its breadth and glory. Speakers and panels will be made up of politicians, economists, architects and of course engineers, all sharing their knowledge and expertise, showcasing the latest innovations and practises.

We are very proud to be able to host this event at Warwick, and provide a platform for discussion such an important topic.

Get Involved

If you are doing research or a project looking into energy or sustainability, we want to hear from you! This will be a fantastic chance to get some exposure for your hard work and earn yourself a £250 cash prize!

One of the main aims of this energy conference is to bring students together to discuss the energy challenges we are going to face in the future. And what could be better than if some students actually showcased how they are trying to solve these problems themselves.

We are looking for individuals or teams to come to the conference and do a 5 minute presentation on their project and discuss how their work is impacting the energy and sustainability industry. These presentation will take place on day of the conference, and in front of all the attendees and an esteemed judging panel, representing industry and academia.
