2016 is the year of travel


STARTUPS ARE DOMINATING...

Uber and AirBnB are the largest companies in the world for taxis and accommodation but startups like Hotel Tonight and Kayak are quickly shaking up other parts of the travel industry too.


MILLENNIALS ARE TRAVELING...

88% of 18 – 30 year olds now travel internationally one to three times a year in search of new and unique experiences and we need new travel services and products to address this growing market.


TECH IS TAKING OVER...

From online bookings to epassports and inflight WiFi the nearly 200M passengers who pass through HK International, Heathrow and JFK every year have never been more digital, connected and mobile.


Personalisation is key...

Building great user experiences has never been more important and with Design Thinking to itterate with customers in real time, so they can solve the real pain points in travel.

The Team

Abbas Kazmi

Abbas is a serial entrepreneur who set up first company at 16 and numerous successful ventures later. Chairman and refounder of the illustrious Oxford Guild Business Society, the largest society in the world, CEO and founder of the Oxford Accelerator and a UK Undergraduate of the Year award winner he has been named as one of the Entrepreneurs of the Year by NACUE and as a 'One to Watch' by Freshminds. He has been the driving force behind some notable events such as Kanye West’s guest lecture at Oxford and the Upstart Word Summit.


- LONDON -

Kristy Hart

An unconventional lawyer and enterprise enthusiast, Kristy has worked alongside the juggernauts of the Global 100 Law Firms on projects like Reuters’ Trust Law Connect and UnLtd’s BVC to support the growth of the startup ecosystem. A founder of HUB Youth Academy and KickstartLDN she has sought to encourage cross-university startup initiatives and is currently organising the International Bar Association’s first student conference in HK, exploring topics like the role of tech and innovation in the legal landscape.

- LONDON -

Johannes Jaeger

Johannes, originally from Germany, has been living in Asia for ten years. During university he represented Hong Kong at the world championships for underwater robotics where his team ranked third place out of 600 teams. Johannes co-founded Plain Exchange, a peer-to-peer foreign exchange platform, which was voted 'Top Fintech Start-up' by Barclays Bank. An active hacker, he and his team won the world's largest hackathon AngelHack, and also organised Europe's largest hackathon Junction and the largest university hackathon in Greater China hackUST.

- HONG KONG -

Kosta Kolev

An engineer by training and entrepreneur by nature, Kosta has worked with a variety of Fortune 500 companies, leading initiatives like HackTrain, StartupBus, and HackTheHouse, bringing together corporates and entrepreneurs to encourage collaborations, discover emerging technologies and nurture new talent. Currently in Hong Kong, he delivered the HKUST Designathon, which culminated in the IoT and Robotics Forum, and is leading MIT Kickstart - the first programme of the wider MIT Hong Kong Innovation Node.
